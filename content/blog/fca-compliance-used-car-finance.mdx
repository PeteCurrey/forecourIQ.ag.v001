Offering finance is essential for any modern independent car dealership. It dramatically increases the affordability of your stock, improves conversion rates, and generates vital commission income. 

However, selling finance in the UK means navigating the stringent rules set by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA). In recent years, the FCA has cracked down heavily on the motor finance sector, particularly focusing on transparency, commission disclosures, and affordability checks.

Here is the 2026 checklist to ensure your dealership stays fully compliant, protecting your business from heavy fines or the loss of your permissions.

## 1. The Consumer Duty

The FCA's "Consumer Duty" regulations fundamentally shifted how dealers must operate. The core principle is that firms must act to deliver "good outcomes" for retail customers.

In practice, this means you can no longer simply point a customer to the finance calculator and ask them to sign. You must ensure they understand the product.

**Compliance Check:**
- Are you clearly explaining the difference between PCP (Personal Contract Purchase) and HP (Hire Purchase)?
- Are you highlighting the risks, such as mileage penalties on a PCP or what happens if they miss a payment?
- Do you have a process to identify "vulnerable customers" and adjust your sales approach accordingly?

## 2. Commission Disclosure Rules

Historically, "Discretionary Commission Arrangements" (where the dealer could adjust the interest rate to earn more commission) were rampant. The FCA banned these entirely to prevent conflicts of interest.

Today, you must operate on a fixed rate or fixed commission model, and crucially, you must be transparent about the fact that you earn a commission.

**Compliance Check:**
- Does your Initial Disclosure Document (IDD) explicitly state that you act as a credit broker, not a lender?
- Does the IDD state that you will receive a commission from the lender for introducing the business?
- If a customer explicitly asks how much commission you are making on their specific deal, you are legally obligated to disclose the exact amount or percentage. Are your sales staff trained to answer this correctly?

## 3. Financial Promotions on Your Website

Your website is a digital showroom, and the FCA regulates the advertising of finance just as strictly online as they do in print or television.

If your website shows a monthly payment figure against a car, it has triggered a "Financial Promotion." This means you must provide a "Representative Example."

**Compliance Check:**
- Does every vehicle detail page with a finance calculator include a clear, prominent Representative Example showing the APR, total amount payable, term length, and any fees?
- Is the APR clearly displayed and marked as "Representative"?
- Are the terms and conditions easily accessible via a single click?

## 4. Affordability and the "Hard Sell"

The FCA is aggressively policing the "hard sell." Customers must be given adequate time to consider the financial commitment they are making.

**Compliance Check:**
- Are you providing customers with the SECCI (Standard European Consumer Credit Information) document *before* they sign the agreement?
- Are you allowing them to take the paperwork away to read, rather than pressuring them to sign on the spot?
- Are you relying on the lender to do all the affordability checks, or are your sales staff actively assessing if the customer seems capable of sustaining the monthly payments?

## 5. Record Keeping and Audit Trails

If the FCA audits your dealership, you must be able to prove that you followed a compliant process for every single finance sale. A verbal agreement is worthless to an auditor.

**Compliance Check:**
- Do you have a digital, time-stamped record of when the IDD and SECCI were provided to the customer?
- Do you use a "Demands and Needs" statement where the customer explicitly agrees that the chosen finance product suits their specific requirements?
- Are these records stored securely in a digital CRM (not in a dusty filing cabinet) for at least six years?

## Summary

FCA compliance shouldn't be viewed as a burden; it is a framework for treating customers fairly. By ensuring your website advertising is transparent, your sales staff are trained on commission disclosure, and your DMS maintains a watertight digital audit trail, you protect your dealership's reputation and its bottom line.
